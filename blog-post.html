The Complete Guide to Santorini:
Best Times, Places & Hidden Gems

AV
Anika Verma
· June 12, 2025 · 8 min read

If Greece is a painting, Santorini is its signature — bold, unmistakable, and impossible to forget. After four visits over six years, I'm convinced it belongs on every traveller's list at least once.

Why Santorini is unlike anywhere else

Santorini didn't always look the way it does today. Around 1600 BCE, one of the largest volcanic eruptions in recorded human history hollowed out the centre of the island, leaving a crescent of cliffs surrounding a flooded caldera. The whitewashed villages that now cling to those cliffs — built centuries later — are essentially perched on the rim of an ancient supervolcano.

That geological drama is what makes the views so impossibly cinematic. From Oia or Imerovigli, you're not looking at a shoreline — you're looking down into the earth, at water 400 metres below sea level, ringed by cliffs that glow golden in the sunset.

"The first time I saw Oia at sunset, I genuinely didn't believe it was real. I thought someone had applied a filter to the sky."

When to go

Santorini is at its most magical in the shoulder seasons. Here's a quick breakdown:

April – JuneWarm days, uncrowded, wildflowers in bloom. Best overall time.
July – AugustPeak season. Hot, busy, and expensive — but the Aegean is warmest for swimming.
September – OctoberOur personal favourite. Crowds thin out but the weather stays warm.
November – MarchMost businesses close. Atmospheric but limited access.

Where to stay

Where you stay in Santorini matters more than almost anywhere else in the world — because the views are the experience. The three main areas each offer a different version of the island.

Oia (Ία)

The most famous village on the island, and for good reason. Oia has the most Instagram-famous windmills, the best sunset viewing spots, and the most boutique hotels. It's also the most expensive and the most crowded. Book at least 6 months in advance for caldera-view hotels in summer.

Fira (Φηρά)

The capital is busier and more commercial than Oia, but it's more central and slightly more affordable. It's also the hub for ferry connections and ATV rentals. The cliff-edge restaurant row has stunning caldera views.

Imerovigli

Perched higher than any other village on the caldera rim, Imerovigli offers arguably the best 360° views in Santorini. Quieter than Oia, more local in character, and only a 30-minute walk between the two villages.

💡Insider Tip

Book a caldera-view hotel even if it's beyond your normal budget. You'll spend more time on that terrace than anywhere else on the island. The view from your private plunge pool at 6 AM, with the caldera below you and no other tourists in sight, is the single best thing about Santorini.

What to do

Beyond the famous sunset, Santorini has more to offer than most people realise.

The Caldera Cruise

A sailing trip around the caldera is a non-negotiable. Most tours stop at the volcanic hot springs (where the water runs red-orange from the iron deposits), the active volcano at Nea Kameni, and then finish with a sunset view from the water looking back at the cliff towns. Book a private catamaran for a group if possible — far better than a large party boat.

Perissa & Perivolos Beaches

The black volcanic sand beaches on the south side of the island are genuinely dramatic. The sand is dark grey-black, the water is crystal clear, and the volcanic cliff of Mesa Vouno towers above you. It's as un-Mediterranean as you can get while still being in Greece.

Wine Tasting

Santorini's volcanic soil produces a truly unique white wine called Assyrtiko — dry, mineral, and citrus-forward. The vines grow in a distinctive basket shape (called a kouloura) to protect them from the wind. Visit Santo Wines for the view, Domaine Sigalas for the best wine.

Practical information

Getting thereDirect flights Athens → Santorini (45 min). Or ferry from Athens Piraeus port (5–8 hours, scenic but long).
Getting aroundRent an ATV (easiest), hire a driver, or use the KTEL buses (cheap but infrequent).
Visa (India)Schengen Tourist Visa required. Apply 4–6 weeks in advance. €80 fee.
CurrencyEuro (€). Most places accept cards; keep some cash for smaller cafes.
LanguageGreek, but English is universally spoken in tourist areas.
Budget₹8,000–₹25,000 per day depending on accommodation tier.

Final thoughts

Santorini can feel like it's been taken over by Instagram — and in peak summer, it has been. But there are quiet versions of this island: a 6 AM walk through Oia before the tour groups arrive, a midweek boat trip in September, a table at a cliffside taverna on a Tuesday night with a carafe of Assyrtiko and the caldera slowly going gold.

That Santorini is still very much there. You just have to time it right.
